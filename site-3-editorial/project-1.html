Transforming Amazon Operations Efficiency with AI, Data and Guided Workspace

Reimagined support issue resolution tool

My Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 9 months - October 2024 to June 2025

Use Case Overview

Transform Amazon's customer issue resolution tool by implementing automated solutions for simple issues while empowering customer service representatives with Agentic workflows and AI-powered Assistants to handle complex customer issues.

Before & After

Before

  • Fragmented experience
  • Excessive scrolling and navigation
  • Poor information hierarchy
  • Communication friction
  • Limited guidance and standardization
  • Cognitive overload
  • Inefficient workflow access

After

  • Issue-specific dynamic layout adaptation
  • Guided resolution with recommended next steps
  • Built-in Associate Assistant with knowledge base integration
  • More intuitive conversational interface
  • Organization and clarity of information flow
  • Quick-access functionality

Impact

22% Reduction in average handling time for complex cases
78% Increase in Associate satisfaction
18% Improvement in first-contact resolution for challenging cases

Users

40K+ Direct - Customer Service Representatives (Associates and Specialists)
2M+ Indirect - Sellers
5K+ Indirect - Supervisors, Advisors, Auditors, Investigators

Use Case Details

Challenges

The existing tool faced several critical issues:

  • Fragmented tooling ecosystem with 180+ tools, 7k+ SOPs, and 25k+ system blurbs
  • Associates spending 51% of handle time researching and executing steps
  • Growing chat volume (32.5%) without optimized tooling
  • Outsourced associates handling 53.2% of contacts with limited tool access
  • Low adoption of LLM tools due to poor integration

Solution

Developed a unified AI-driven workspace with dynamic guided workflows and three key panels:

Diagnose Panel

Features LLM Issue Summary and Associate Assistant for guided support

Solve Panel

Centralized workspace with execution widget, key insights diagnostics, and activity Timeline

Respond Panel

Automated response generation and communication management

Goals

The 2025 Associate Experience transformation initiative aims to revolutionize Amazon's Seller Support platform with three key measurable goals: drive adoption of LLM-powered tools across a target percentage of cases by year-end, successfully launch an integrated LLM-powered interface, and achieve specific operational excellence metrics. These goals support the broader vision of creating a unified, intelligent workspace that reduces the current 51% handle time spent on research and execution, improves chat handling efficiency from the current 15.1-minute ACHT, and lowers transfer rates from the present 16.02% for outsourced associates. The initiative will eliminate the fragmentation of 180+ tools while providing seamless integration with upstream automation capabilities, ultimately enabling associates to handle more complex cases efficiently while maintaining quality standards and reducing security risks from unauthorized workarounds.

Goal 1

Goal 2

Usability Testing

We led multiple rounds of usability tests with Seller Support associates from India and Costa Rica. Participants tackled real-life complex cases using interactive prototypes, while we observed their workflows, pain points, and moments of confusion. The research revealed which features accelerated resolution and which introduced friction. Associates provided feedback on interface clarity and feature discoverability. These insights directly informed final refinements before launch.

Problem 1

Associates struggled to understand how the Assistant worked with the workspace

Solution 1

Learnings

  • Establish a training module for the new platform
  • Iterative prototyping uncovers usability issues that requirements alone miss.
  • Clear, context-rich interfaces have an outsized impact on user confidence in complex scenarios.

Next Steps

  • Roll out advanced analytics to surface emerging case trends for associates.
  • Expand collaboration tools to support even more cross-team coordination.
  • Continue regular usability check-ins post-launch to drive continuous improvement.
